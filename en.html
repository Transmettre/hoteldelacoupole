City monument

English

Audioguide:


The former Hôtel de la Coupole is located in the very heart of Soissons, on Rue du Chaperon-Rouge, just steps from the cathedral and directly opposite the covered market.
Built around 1923, after the destruction of the First World War, it reflects the city’s reconstruction period.
The project was led by Émile Naudin, a metalworker from Soissons, and carried out by the architect Marc Solotareff, a Russian refugee following the 1917 Revolution, who settled in Soissons with his family in 1921.
The building, made of stone and zinc, with brick at the rear, adopts a neo-Renaissance style combined with Art Deco motifs and materials. Its features include a symmetrical façade, small round windows known as “oeil-de-boeuf” (bull’s-eye windows), and above all its two distinctive domes, which gave the building its name.
Over time, the site has had several uses: a shop, a brasserie, then a hotel-brasserie from 1935 onward, linked to the development of memorial tourism, and even a greengrocer’s shop.
Today, it is privately owned and still houses shops on the ground floor.
